# African Climate Vulnerability Analysis for COP32

## Executive Summary
Analysis of NASA POWER climate data (2015-2026) across Ethiopia, Kenya, Sudan, Tanzania, and Nigeria to identify key climate trends ahead of COP32 in Addis Ababa.

## Key Findings

### Temperature Trends
Sudan shows the highest mean temperatures and fastest warming trend among the five countries.

### Precipitation Variability
Sudan exhibits the most unstable rainfall patterns, while Ethiopia and Tanzania show relatively stable precipitation.

### Extreme Events
Sudan experiences the most extreme heat days (T2M_MAX > 35°C), while prolonged dry spells affect multiple countries.

## Vulnerability Ranking
| Rank | Country | Rationale |
|------|---------|-----------|
| 1 | Sudan | Highest temperatures, most variable rainfall, most extreme heat days |
| 2 | Nigeria | Rising temperatures, moderate-high variability |
| 3 | Kenya | Moderate temperatures, moderate variability |
| 4 | Ethiopia | Stable temperatures, consistent rainfall |
| 5 | Tanzania | Most stable climate patterns |

## 5 COP32 Recommendations
1. **Sudan is warming fastest** — demands urgent adaptation finance for heat-resilient agriculture
2. **Sudan has the most unstable precipitation** — requires investment in water infrastructure
3. **Extreme heat and drought are compounding** — loss and damage mechanisms must recognize compound events
4. **Ethiopia shows relative climate stability** — positions it as a credible regional leader for COP32
5. **Sudan should be prioritized for climate finance** — highest composite vulnerability score

## Conclusion
Africa's climate vulnerabilities are differentiated, demanding targeted finance and adaptation strategies. Ethiopia's COP32 leadership can amplify Africa's voice in global negotiations.
